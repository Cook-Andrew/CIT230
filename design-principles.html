White Space

Jazz FM Romania
https://jazzfm.ro/

While it may be better to call it "Yellow Space" in this case, this website exemplifies clean design. There is only once function you absolutely need to listen to the radio: a play button. It's reasonable for the company to want to place their logo, and a now playing ribbon to the screen as well. That's it! Three elements is all the website needs.

Visual Hierarchy

CNN
https://cnn.com/

Through the use of h1 through h3 headers, CNN is able to create a visual hierarchy. Just based off the boldness of the words I am able to tell the designer thought that the title was most important, the subtitle was next most important, and the related articles are the least important. The words "breaking news" do not follow the header pattern but you can tell that they are also important because of the red background. The logo and the menu are the smalled elements on the screen. Despite the related articles being the least important as individuals, as a group they take up the most space and subtly become most important element because the news agency wants you to spend more time on the website. Their goal, after all, is conversion.

Fitt's law

Amazon
https://amazon.com/

Ignoring the dorky title for the wishlist I created for my wife to buy me Valentine's day presents, this list demonstrates Amazon's strong use of Fitt's law. The most important button for profits is "Add to Cart" which is largest and in the center of the screen. The next most important button for making money is "+ invite". While this button doesn't directly translate to a sale like the previous example, this button attracts new customers to look at the product. It is slightly farther away from the center, but it is quite large compared to other buttons on the screen. "View Lists" is tiny and the far top of the screen. This button is necessary for function, but will not make the company any money, so they put it out of the way.
